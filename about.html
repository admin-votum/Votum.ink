Votum · About
Built for the reader.
Not the algorithm.
The problem

Most political news is optimized for engagement. Headlines are engineered to provoke, not inform. Articles are padded to justify the click. The actual fact — the thing you came to find out — is buried somewhere in paragraph seven.

Meanwhile every feed, every platform, every outlet has a thumb on the scale. Sometimes intentional. Sometimes structural. Often invisible even to the people running it.

The result is a reader who speed-scrolls looking for the one bolded word that answers the question, already knowing roughly what the article will say before they open it.

That is not information. That is noise with a headline.

What Votum does

Votum aggregates political news from across the full spectrum — left, center, and right — labels every source transparently, and gets out of the way.

No algorithmic amplification. No engagement ranking. No outrage economics. Articles are sorted by freshness and source credibility. The reader sees everything. The reader decides.

How it works
1
Aggregate
News is pulled simultaneously from multiple sources across the political spectrum and merged into a single feed, deduplicated and sorted by time.
2
Label
Every article is tagged with its source's political lean. Not to discredit — to inform. A colored pip tells you where the outlet sits on the spectrum before you read a word.
Left Center-left Center Center-right Right
3
Analyze
Each article is analyzed for source credibility, coverage spread across the spectrum, and editorial flags where applicable.
Emotionally charged language Single source cited Missing opposing view Story covered by one side only
4
The Point
Every article is reduced to its single true sentence. The actual fact, stripped of scaffolding. What happened, in plain language, under 25 words.
5
Counter story
Where coverage is one-sided, Votum surfaces the opposing perspective on the same event. Not to create false balance — to ensure completeness.
The goal

The hardest bias to design out is your own. Votum is built toward maximum autonomy — a platform that surfaces information cleanly, labels it honestly, and introduces as little human editorial judgment as possible.

The reader is trusted. The reader is capable. The reader does not need to be told what to think about what they just read.

That trust is the whole product.

The name

Votum — Latin for the formal expression of a citizen's will. The word Romans used for the declaration made before the assembly. A vow, a vote, a wish made public.

.ink — the news. And the permanence of what's written.
